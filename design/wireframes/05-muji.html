V論
The Point — An Essayfor the non-technical reader
essay · 3 paragraphs

A dumber system would have written twenty-two wrong line items into our ERP and left a human to discover the mess a week later — usually the week the customer calls asking why the wrong parts arrived at the wrong dock. This agent refused. It flagged the email, opened an exception with a full audit trail, and drafted a clarification that reads like it was written by a careful purchasing coordinator on their second coffee. That refusal is the feature.

Automation, in this domain, is not about speed. It is about the quality of the writes you let through. The orders collection is the system of record; everything downstream — shipping, invoicing, inventory forecasts — trusts it. An agent that floods that collection with plausible-looking garbage is worse than no agent at all. An agent that knows when to stop, and how to ask, is the point.

We built the eight-stage pipeline so every refusal is legible: the exception record carries the email, the extracted lines, the failed match attempts per tier, the confidence arithmetic, and the drafted reply. When the operator picks it up, the work is already done — all they approve is the send.

“An agent that knows when to stop, and how to ask, is the point.”
